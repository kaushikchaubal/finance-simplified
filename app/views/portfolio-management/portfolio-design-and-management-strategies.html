Strategic Asset Allocation Strategic asset allocation sets an investor’s desired long-term exposures to systematic (market) risk. Expectations involved in strategic asset allocation are long term. “Long term” has different interpretations for different investors, but five years is a reasonable minimum reference point. Tactical Asset Allocation Tactical asset allocation involves making short-term adjustments to asset-class weights based on short-term predictions of relative performance among asset classes. TAA can subsume a range of approaches, from occasional and ad hoc adjustments to frequent and model-based adjustments.
