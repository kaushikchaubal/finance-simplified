Macroeconomic Principles Main principles of macro-economics and macro policy: Economic growth is desirable: expansion is better than contraction Economies have a potential growth rate, determined by the supply of ‘factors of production’ (labour, capital, land) and technology (which determines the productivity of these resources) Full employment – the situation where everyone who wants to work has a job – is desirable Low, stable inflation is better than high inflation or deflation Control of inflation is best left to independent central banks Fiscal policy can be used to stabilise economies, but there are important limits on debt and deficits International free trade is desirable.
