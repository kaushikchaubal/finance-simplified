Key macro-economic measures: GDP Gross Domestic product – the total output of an economy Money supply – the amount of money (cash and bank accounts) in circulation Employment – the number of people employed Unemployment rate – the proportion of the workforce unemployed Retail sales – sales of retail goods such as food, fuel and retail services such as restaurant meals Inflation – measured by the change, normally over a twelve month period, in one of the main measures of prices (such as the CPI, consumer price index Government debt – the total debt accumulated by the government over time Balance of payments – the difference between exports and imports
